What counts as a Carbohydrate
Carbohydrates are foods that give our bodies energy. Carbohydrate foods are things like bread, pasta, and desserts. Carbodyrates also include fruits, milk, yogurt and the starchy vegetables:
  • Potatoes
  • Sweet potatoes
  • Peas
  • Corn
  • Winter squashes (example: acorn and spaghetti)
  • Beans*
  • Lentils*
    •
    What counts as a Protein
    Protein foods are important for growth and development, and they're important for strong bones and muscles. Protein foods include:
  • All meats
  • All seafood
  • Eggs
  • Nuts and Seeds
  • Tofu
  • Plain Greek Yogurt
    •
    What counts as a Vegetable
    Vegetables are filled with vitamins, minerals, fiber and anti-oxidants. They're low in carbohdryates and calories and are really important for a healthy and balanced food plan. Aim for half of the plate as a vegetable. Vegetables include:
  • Summer squash (example: zucchini)
  • Carrots
  • Celery
  • Tomatoes
  • Broccoli
  • Cauliflower
  • Dark green leafy vegetables (kale, spinach, collard greens, beet greens)
  • Romaine lettuce
  • Eggplant
  •
    Notes

    *For vegetarian and vegan food plans, beans and lentils count as both a carbohydrate AND a protein.

    Choosing carbs with more protein and less carbs can help control blood sugar. Using spaghetti squash instead of pasta offers a more balanced carb option.
