Menu Madness



Why it's important
If you've been to any chain restaurant lately, you might have noticed a lot more numbers next to your food options. Most restaurants are now required to post nutrition information about their foods right on the menus. But does this information really make a difference in health? Studies are showing that giving consumers this information isn't having the hoped for results - people choosing the "healthier" options are the ones that tend to choose healthier foods anyway.

What you can do
1. If you're going out only every once and awhile and use it as a treat, go ahead and enjoy. Having a splurge meal every once and awhile is just fine.

2. If you're eating out more than every once and awhile: stick to the under 500 calorie (ideally under 400) or "heart-healthy" menu items and get a vegetable side-dish if it doesn't come with one. Most meals, even the "healthy" ones, don't include many vegetables.

3. Share a menu item with a friend. Choose an additional vegetable side dish so you both can have a balanced meal.

4. Focus on making your other meals on the days you eat out healthy. That way you make sure you have an overall healthy and balanced day.
