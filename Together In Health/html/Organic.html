Does organic really mean organic?



Why it's important
In the past decade, organic foods have been flooding the market, marketed as healthier than their conventional counterparts, and often at a much steeper price. So what is all the hype about organic about? Are they really healthier for you? In terms of nutrition content, organic and non-organic foods have, in general, the same nutritional content. Organic farmers are allowed to use pesticides, and in fact, they do. The difference between organic and non-organic is not the use of pesticides, it is where the pesticides come from, and organic foods must not contain any GMOs. Organic pesticides have to come from a natural product, they can't be made in a lab. But just because it's natural, doesn't mean it's healthy or safe. The best way to ensure you're getting a safe product that was sustainably grown is to ask the farmer. Often times, organic food shipped from another country is actually worse for the environment than a non-organic local food. There are a lot of non-organic farmers in the US who aren't certified, so be a detective, and know what goes on your food before it gets to you.

What you can do
Check out local farmer's markets or CSA's and try to start making your produce local produce. Now that spring is here and summer is upon us, we'll be seeing all sorts of great local produce.
