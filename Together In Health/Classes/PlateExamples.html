Plate examples
Pizza

If you're making it at home you can control the fat content, if it's greasy make sure to add a "greasy/fried" plate for that meal as well.

Deep dish pizza with meat topping and no vegetables.


Deep dish pizza with meat topping and vegetable topping.


Thin crust pizza with protein and vegetable topping, with a side salad, counts as a balanced meal.


Spaghetti

Spaghetti with sauce but no protein.


Spaghetti with meat sauce, or spaghetti and meatballs.


Spaghetti and protein with a side of vegetables counts as a balanced plate.


Casseroles

Casserole dishes, especially when you didn't make it, can be hard to figure out what plate it looks like. Check out the tip below for help finding the plate that most resembles your meal:

  • If there are vegetables, a protein and carbs in the casserole, compare the amount of vegetables to the amount of protein and carbs in the casserole. For a casserole that counts as a balanced plate, there should be twice as many vegetables as carbs and protein, and about equal amounts of carbs and protein.
