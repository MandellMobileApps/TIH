Being Ergonomically Correct



Why it's important
Ergonomics is a topic we don't always associate with our overall health and wellbeing. But the way we sit, hold our phone and even type can affect our entire bodies. Hunching over or sitting at an angle can cause neck, back, shoulder, hip and leg pain, it can also lead to digestive problems. Not having proper lighting means we can strain our eyes, causing headaches. Don't sit with your knees at a 90 degree angle? You could be causing knee damage. Check out the resource below for ways to make your office space more ergonomically friendly. And unwind after a long day with yoga. Yoga helps stretch out all your muscles and realign your back, leading to you feeling much better. Just 5 minutes a night is enough to improve your flexibility. And having good flexibility is one of the most important things we can have as we get older. Yoga originated in India, and there are many different schools of yoga, including Hatha, Raja and Vinyasa yoga. Yoga become popular in the west in the 1980's as a new exercise, but the origins of yoga were for meditative and spiritual purposes.

What you can do
Strike a pose with yoga. Yoga is one of the best ways to begin the day fresh and stretch everything out at the end of a long day. Aim for 5 minutes every night before bed. Set up an ergonomically correct workspace. Much of the aches and pains we feel are actually due to poor workspace set-ups.
